Beyond the Screen:
    I didn't begin my journey as a programmer with a clear plan. At first, it was simply curiosity — a desire to understand what really happens behind the screen. Over time, that curiosity turned into practice, and practice slowly became a habit.
    I enjoy building things from scratch and embracing the challenges that come with debugging and failure. Along the way, I met Xiaoyun Li, Xinshuo Ji, Wei Li, Zichen Wang, Bo Qu, and Chang Wei. They are more than classmates or collaborators to me; they are friends, even family. Programming, for me, is not just about writing code. It's a way to train my thinking, solve problems, and learn patience.
      This blog started with a simple idea — I wanted a place to document what I'm learning, how I think, and how I grow. It doesn't need to be perfect; it just needs to be honest and allow me to move forward, step by step. For a long time, though, I didn't have a clear direction. That changed during my internship at Boeing. There, I met Eric Zhang, who studied abroad and brought a completely different perspective. He inspired me deeply, helped me strengthen my weaknesses, shared experiences and ways of thinking that no textbook could teach, and showed me what real depth looks like.
        Another person who had a profound impact on me at Boeing was my senior manager, Ben Dou. When I felt uncertain about my future career, he gave me guidance at a critical moment. Unlike teachers in school who often speak in abstract terms, he offered practical advice tailored to my personality and the way I communicate. His guidance played an important role in shaping my understanding of my future path and the kind of work I hope to create and leave behind.
      Finally, I sincerely thank everyone who has helped me along the way, and my parents for their unwavering support. Thank you all for allowing me to continue moving forward on this path.
